Chess Versus Go!

By Virginia Moreno Diaz

I like to play chess against the computer although I never win.  I use  Cool Math Games to play . So I wanted to write an article to see if I could get some students into chess and then try to beat them. Mr. M. and I were talking about what should Go in the article, and I invited him to play me!

I didn't win against Mr. M. either, but he showed me another game, which is much more simple but also very hard, called Go, which comes from Japan.  I still didn't beat him, but I love the game and now we are playing each other at Online Go. Here's what our game looks like:

Here is how Go works:   In Go you have to surround your opponent’s pegs with your pegs to try and capture your opponent’s pegs. Unlike Chess in Go you capture as many of your opponents  pegs as you are able to surround. In Go black always goes first. You can play Go online as well. Go you capture the number of pegs of your opponent that you are able to surround and In chess you can only capture one of your opponent's pegs at a time.
