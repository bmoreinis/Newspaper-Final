Moaning Myrtle: Another Rowling Rip-Off?

by Sue Dogirl

Gandalf reborn as Dumbledore.

At least Rowling reached outside England's Lord of the Rings this time.

Many have remarked on how Lord of the Rings characters make their appearance in JK Rowling's Harry Potter series . But have you ever noticed how Moaning Myrtle is a total copy of anime bathroom ghost Hanako-san?But have you ever noticed how Moaning Myrtle is a total copy of anime bathroom ghost Hanako-san?

Hanako-san, as depicted in the anime Gakkou no Kowai Uwasa Shin: Hanako-san ga Kita!!

Moaning Myrtle, as depicted in the Harry Potter movie series by actress Shirley Henderson

As documented in Wikipedia, Japanese urban jegend Hanako-san has quite similar origins and behaviors to Moaning Myrtle, as documented in the Harry Potter fandom wiki:

    1. Moaning Myrtle and Hanako-san are both spirits of young girls who haunt school toilets.
    2. Moaning Myrtle and Hanako-san were both bullied by their classmates.
    3. Both died hiding in the bathroom.
    4. Both were suicidal, although they were actually murdered.

Rowling, of course, denies this charge of plagiarism. Instead, she claims that Moaning Myrtle was inspired by girls who cry in bathrooms. As documented by an on-point blog post by Anita Wirawan,. Rowling explains:

"The inspiration for Moaning Myrtle was the frequent presence of a crying girl in communal bathrooms, especially at the parties and discos of my youth."

But how much credibility should we give Rowling, after stealing so much from Lord of the Rings to make a more modern version for today's kids? I don't know about you, but when I want to include something someone else wrote in my work, I always give full credit.

Still, I guess Rowling has a point. If you're a teenage girl, or know one, you know that things to cry about come up fairly frequently, and there are no private (or semi-private) places to go and bawl besides the bathroom (unless you want to have a talk with the school psychologist if she's available).

I don't know what memories and associations teenage boys have with their counterpart spaces -- and I really do not want to know, trust me! -- but for those of us who have gone through this, the urban legend of a crying girl haunting school bathrooms really hits home.

I guess that's the real point of this article, and why I wanted to write it.
